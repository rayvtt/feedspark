Case Studies / Test Wins

Reiss' own tests in flight, plus the proof already banked on comparable FeedSpark accounts.

Reiss — Local Language case study

Local Language vs. English Shopping campaigns, tested across 16 EU/ME markets. This is a structural test on how much translated, market-native copy actually moves performance versus running English campaigns everywhere — a question specific to a 29-market fashion retailer, not a generic playbook item.

Reiss — Title de-duplication + Search Intent A/B

39% of titles were duplicated before the auto-optimised-title fix; the [Search Intent] vs. none A/B test that followed is reading now. Two structural, sequential tests on the same lever — title quality — rather than one-off experiments.

Proof from comparable brands

What's already proven on other FeedSpark accounts

Same testing methodology, same title-quality thesis — run on other clients ahead of Reiss, not projected numbers.

Brand inclusion in titles

Adding the brand name to the title measurably lifted CTR on a comparable FeedSpark account — the same structural lever Reiss' title work is built on.

Benefit copy in titles

+24.7% CTR from naming the benefit directly in the title rather than leaving it implicit — the same "spell it out, don't imply it" principle behind the Search Intent test.

Full-word spelling

+126.6% CTR from spelling a term out in full rather than abbreviating it — evidence that decomposable, unambiguous titles consistently outperform compressed ones.

Chapter nine

Tech Fit

Why the FeedSpark stack fits a 29-market fashion retailer specifically.

One stack, every market

Tachyon AI + FeedHero cover generation and management end to end — one build, not 29 separate market-by-market projects.

Proven across fashion

The same title-quality methodology behind Reiss' de-duplication fix and Search Intent test is already proven across fashion, footwear, beauty and outdoor accounts.

Golden Record discipline

99.9% attribute-completeness target across every feed dimension — the 88.6% figure from the last audit is the checkpoint against that target, not the ceiling.

One partner per channel

Google Shopping/PMax, Meta, social and LIA all run off the same optimisation engine — no separate vendor per surface as Reiss' channel mix grows.

The through-line

Every proven lever this year — de-duplicated titles, the Keyword Planner, custom labels at 75% — is the same fuller, more decomposable data pattern that Tier 2 and the conversational attributes scale up next. It's the next step on the line already being walked, not a new strategy.
