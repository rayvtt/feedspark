The AI landscape — why this matters now

Dog owners don't search anymore. They ask. And three separate pipelines decide whether YuMOVE is in the answer.

The shift

Search is shifting — from 10 blue links to AI answers

"My 11-year-old lab is stiff getting up in the morning, what should I give her?" — that query doesn't match a keyword. It matches a product whose feed can answer it.

Zero-click is accelerating

AI overviews, chat-led answers and agentic assistants resolve queries without a click. More shoppers get the answer inside the interface — your product is either in that answer, or it doesn't exist.

The model picks the product

LLMs read structured feeds, attributes and reviews to decide what to recommend. Thin data means YuMOVE never enters the consideration set — regardless of brand strength or ad spend.

Supplements are answer-shaped

Dosage, life stage, breed size, onset time, active ingredient. Every purchase decision is a question. And questions are exactly what these new AI surfaces answer.

The black box problem

Three pipelines feed the AI — you can't control which one it uses

A query about dog joint supplements might be answered from training data, a live web crawl, or your Merchant Center feed. The only way to win is to ensure all three pipelines contain rich, accurate, consistent product data.

Google Gemini / AI Mode

The primary pipeline for Google surfaces
  • Pulls from Google Merchant Center feeds
  • Shopping Graph (50B+ listings)
  • Conversational attributes (new — chapter 06)
  • Direct Offers for checkout inside AI Mode
  • Business Agent for brand-voice chat

ChatGPT Shopping

Direct merchant feed integration
  • CSV, TSV, XML, JSON feed formats accepted
  • Updates accepted every 15 minutes
  • Instant Checkout via Stripe (US)
  • 1M+ Shopify merchants in pipeline
  • Shopping overlay doesn't render — only image + title + price

Perplexity

Aggregates from marketplace feeds
  • Shopify, Amazon, BigCommerce feeds
  • Merchant programme for higher visibility
  • Visual product cards with pros/cons
  • Virtual try-on for applicable categories
  • Crawls PDPs — structured data on-site matters
The practical point for YuMOVE: your Shopping overlay — the SUB-G roundel — works in Google PLAs. It does not render inside ChatGPT Shopping. In ChatGPT, the AI agent reads your title, description, and attributes to decide whether to recommend you. That's why the attribute build in chapter 06 is the lever, not another overlay.
Agentic commerce

Three protocols that let AI agents shop on behalf of consumers

These are emerging open standards. None requires immediate action from YuMOVE — but they explain why the attribute build matters: the agents need structured data to act.

UCP — Universal Commerce Protocol

Google, Shopify, Target, Walmart

Common language for AI commerce
  • Enables agents to handle discounts, loyalty, subscriptions
  • Complete checkout inside the AI conversation
  • Activated via native_commerce flag in Merchant Center
  • Relevant now: YuMOVE's Subscribe & Save mechanic maps here
MCP — Model Context Protocol

Anthropic, adopted by Shopify

Real-time data access for AI assistants
  • Live inventory, pricing, order status via API
  • Shopify merchants get automatic MCP endpoints
  • Any AI assistant speaking MCP can access store data
  • Relevant later: depends on YuMOVE's platform architecture
ACP — Agentic Commerce Protocol

OpenAI (powers ChatGPT Shopping)

Instant checkout inside ChatGPT
  • Integrates with Stripe, PayPal
  • Users complete purchases without leaving the conversation
  • Currently US-only for checkout
  • Relevant now: discovery is global even if checkout isn't

The through-line across all three

Every protocol assumes the agent can read structured product data — attributes, Q&A, related products, variant options. The feed isn't just a shopping ad input anymore. It's the API that AI agents call to reason about your product.

The Google Shopping → ChatGPT pipeline

A March 2026 study of 43,000 ChatGPT shopping carousel products found that 83% match Google Shopping's top 40 organic listings, with 60% from the top 10. This means optimising the Google Merchant Center feed is simultaneously optimising for ChatGPT visibility.

ChatGPT's reliance on product feeds has climbed every month — from 4.3% of all shopping retrievals six months ago to roughly 20% today. Brands without feed integration are increasingly invisible to ChatGPT shoppers.

Sources: MediaFast (Mar 2026), Profound (Jun 2026)

Health is the most AI-disrupted category

Ahrefs data shows 43% of health-related queries now trigger an AI Overview — the highest of any sector. E-commerce/shopping sits at only 3.2% overall, but health-adjacent shopping queries (supplements, vitamins, pet healthcare) inherit the health sector's higher AI trigger rate.

Queries of 8+ words are 7× more likely to trigger an AI Overview. "Best joint supplement for senior labrador with hip dysplasia" is exactly the length and specificity that triggers the AI surface — and exactly the query YuMOVE should be answering.

Sources: Ahrefs (2026), WordStream, Digital Applied

Theories

How conversational attributes could compound across surfaces

None of these are proven — they're hypotheses drawn from the data above, each one testable within the Q3/Q4 programme.

Theory 1: Q&A → AI citation loop

If question_and_answer provides the AI agent with a pre-structured answer, the agent can cite YuMOVE directly rather than inferring from a description. Cited brands get 35% more organic clicks. The compound effect: more clicks → better Shopping Graph signal → higher organic Shopping rank → more AI citations.

Test: Measure AI Overview citation rate for SKUs with Q&A vs without, using AI visibility tracking (Profound or Otterly AI).

Theory 2: Variant options → range capture

When a user asks "do you do this in a bigger pack?", the agent needs variant_option to answer without sending the user away. If YuMOVE's variant data is clean, the agent can recommend across the range in one answer — capturing the sale that would otherwise go to whichever competitor has cleaner variant data.

Test: Compare cross-sell rate for products with item_group_title + variant_option vs products without.

Theory 3: Popularity rank → agent confidence

AI agents use ranking signals to break ties. popularity_rank is a direct, first-party signal that says "this is our best-seller." When the agent compares YuMOVE Joint Care (popularity_rank: 95.5) against an unranked competitor, the ranked product carries higher citation confidence.

Test: A/B test impression share for SKUs with popularity_rank populated vs control group.

Theory 4: Document links → authority signal

For health products, agents need evidence — not just claims. document_link pointing to clinical summaries or ingredient breakdowns gives the agent something to cite as authoritative. Google's Shopping Graph already cross-references structured data; PDFs give it a deeper evidence base to draw from.

Test: Monitor whether Google AI Mode cites YuMOVE's linked documents in health-related overviews.

Theory 5: Description richness → long-tail capture

Descriptions under 200 chars match almost nothing in long-tail. At 500+ chars with use-case language, the description becomes the semantic field the agent matches against. Structured data on PDPs lifts organic CTR ~30%. The feed description is the same lever applied at Shopping Graph scale.

Test: Measure impression volume for 8+ word queries before and after description expansion.

Theory 6: Feed completeness → PMax efficiency

PMax campaigns depend heavily on feed quality. Google's own documentation states that optional attributes give the algorithm more signal for matching. The shift from keyword-matching to attribute-matching means every unpopulated field is impression share left on the table — and the conversational attributes are six new fields the competition hasn't populated either.

Test: Track PMax performance metrics (CPA, ROAS) as attribute completeness increases through Q3.

The testing advantage: Google's product data experiments now support controlled A/B testing on feed attributes — including in PMax. We can validate each theory with a randomised split and a supplemental feed, the same methodology Boston Proper used. Every test generates a result whether the hypothesis holds or not.

Chapter five

Tachyon for supplements

FeedSpark's AI engine — adapted from fashion to the pet-health category. Three capabilities, one pipeline.

Capability 1

Long-intent search-term generation

Content engineered for AI search agents — not just keyword matching. The shift: generative engines resolve conversational, intent-rich queries, not keywords.

01

Feed extraction

Tachyon reads live YuMOVE product data — title, description, active ingredients, life stage, format, price.

02

Intent generation

LLM generates 20–40 long-tail intent phrases per SKU. "joint supplement for senior labrador under £40", "glucosamine chews for stiff older dogs".

03

Scoring & validation

Generated terms scored against search-term reports + Google Trends signals. Winners selected per SKU.

04

Feed injection

Winning terms embedded in title, description, product highlights. Supplemental feed — primary feed untouched.

YuMOVE example — Joint Care PLUS Max Strength

Input: Glucosamine · Green-lipped mussel · Senior dogs · 120 tablets · £52.99 · Joint support

AI-generated intent terms:
"strongest joint supplement for old dogs with arthritis"
"glucosamine and green-lipped mussel for large breed dogs"
"senior dog mobility supplement under £55"
"vet-recommended joint support for stiff ageing dogs"
"chewable joint tablets for dogs who won't eat pills"
// 20-40 generated, top 4-6 used per SKU

Why this matters for supplements specifically

In fashion, Tachyon matches terms like "smart-casual blazer for summer wedding." For supplements, the queries are health questions with a purchase intent behind them — and they're far more specific:

"My 8-year-old cockapoo is limping after walks" → this is a query an AI agent will resolve with a product recommendation. YuMOVE wins that recommendation only if the feed carries the life stage, the symptom language, and the ingredient data the agent needs to reason with.

The title tests already proved the principle: spelling out benefits (+24.7% CTR) and ingredients beat abbreviated copy. Tachyon scales that across the full catalogue.

Capability 2

Description enrichment — the AI's briefing document

Your description is what the AI agent reads to decide whether YuMOVE answers the question. It should read like advice from a knowledgeable vet nurse — not a keyword list.

Intent matching

❌ "YuMOVE Joint Care for Dogs. Contains glucosamine. 60 tablets."
✓ "Clinically proven joint supplement for dogs showing early signs of stiffness. Contains veterinary-grade glucosamine and green-lipped mussel. Most owners report visible improvement within 4–6 weeks of daily feeding. Tablet format — can be crushed into food for fussy eaters."

Use-case scenarios

❌ "Supports joint health and mobility."
✓ "Ideal for dogs who are slowing down on walks, struggling with stairs, or stiff after rest. Particularly suited to medium-to-large breeds from age 7+. Use alongside YuMOVE Skin & Coat for dogs with both joint and skin concerns."

Comparative context

❌ "Premium quality joint supplement."
✓ "Sits between our everyday Joint Care and the vet-strength PLUS Max Strength. Contains the same Green-Lipped Mussel extract as the PLUS range but at a maintenance dose. Upgrade to PLUS if your dog is in active discomfort."

Pre-answered Q&A

❌ "See product page for more information."
✓ "How many tablets? 1 per day for small dogs (up to 15kg), 2 for medium (15–30kg), 3 for large (30kg+). Can I give it with other supplements? Yes — safe alongside YuMOVE digestive range. 60-tablet pack lasts 1–2 months depending on dog size."
Efficiency note from Google's spec: anything carried in description, product_highlight or product_detail doesn't need duplicating into the conversational attributes. So the description expansion and the attribute build are the same content exercise — not two separate workstreams.
Capability 3

Visual attribute harvest

Tachyon reads the product image and feed data, then extracts structured attributes automatically. For Reiss that means silhouette, neckline, sleeve length. For YuMOVE, it means something different.

01

Image + feed ingested

Pack image, existing title, description, and category data fed into Tachyon.

02

Vision analysis

AI reads the pack: format (tablet/chew/bites), life stage icon, active-ingredient callout, pack count, brand sub-line.

03

Attribute generation

15–25 structured attributes per SKU: format, count, life_stage, target_condition, key_ingredient, flavour.

04

Validation

Each attribute verified against feed data. Flagged if confidence is low — nothing goes live unchecked.

Why this matters for AI discovery

Product images in the shopping feed are no longer shown only in PLAs and Free Listings — they also appear in ChatGPT Shopping and Google AI Mode. But there's a catch:

Overlays don't render in ChatGPT. An apparel brand ran a PPC overlay and saw +16% CTR and +33% conversions — but only in Google Shopping. In ChatGPT, the agent reads the feed data behind the image, not the image itself.

The visual harvest closes this gap: attributes Tachyon reads from the pack image get written back into the feed as structured data — making the product discoverable even when the image isn't displayed.

Probability ranking

High confidence — T1 (ingredient titles), T2 (descriptions), T6 (seasonal KWs). These follow directly from proven YuMOVE tests and the Boston Proper result. Expected uplift: +10–25% CTR.

Medium confidence — T3, T4, T5, T9, T10. Structural attributes with strong logic but no YuMOVE-specific precedent. Expected uplift: +5–15% impression share.

Exploratory — T7, T8, T11, T13, T14. AI citation measurement is new territory. The tests are valuable even if the uplift is initially small — they establish the baseline we measure everything else against.

Compound effect model

The tests aren't independent — they compound. Description enrichment (T2) makes Q&A (T7) more effective because the agent has two sources of structured data instead of one. Variant options (T3) make related products (T5) more useful because the agent can navigate the range. Popularity rank (T4) amplifies everything by giving the agent a confidence signal.

Conservative estimate: if each layer adds 5–8% incremental visibility, the compound effect across all six conversational attributes + description enrichment could deliver 25–40% total uplift in AI-surface impressions by Q1 2027.

The measurement gap

Traditional search console data doesn't capture AI citations. T14 addresses this: we need a tool (Profound, Otterly AI, or manual tracking) to measure how often YuMOVE appears in ChatGPT recommendations, Google AI Mode answers, and Perplexity product cards.

This is the single biggest risk to the programme — not that the attribute build won't work, but that we won't be able to prove it worked. Recommending budget for AI visibility tracking alongside the attribute build.

Worth noting: 95% of merchants see AI agent traffic on their sites, but only 20% have machine-readable catalogues (PayPal, Consensus Miami, May 2026). The gap is the opportunity. Every attribute YuMOVE populates that competitors don't is a structural advantage that compounds as AI surfaces grow.
