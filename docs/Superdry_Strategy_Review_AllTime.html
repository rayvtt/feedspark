Chapter two

The delivery stack

The tools doing the work behind the Superdry programme.

Unit / tech stack

Four layers, mapped to what Superdry actually uses

Every layer below is live on the account today — this isn't a capability menu, it's the machinery already running the 19-market feed.

FeedHero

Feed management & rules engine

Range Completion exclusion, Hero Sizes rule, custom labels CL0–CL4 (margin, low-performer, seasonal, bestseller), keyword injection, image-overlay rules, output-feed exclusions and per-market batch set-up.

Tachyon AI

LLM enrichment engine

Search-intent generation for keyword optimisation (Jackets, Coats, Dresses, Shorts), description enrichment, and visual attribute harvest from product imagery — the engine behind the title and keyword tests.

Scrapers & supplemental

Data acquisition

Sale line-list ingestion, margin-label data, zombie-SKU detection (0 impressions / 30 days), competitor keyword capture and supplemental feeds for data-field back-filling.

Output channels

Where the feed lands

Google Shopping & PMax, Meta / Social DPA, and Local Inventory Ads (in progress) — each market's feed built once, then split and localised per channel.

The point of the stack: the same enrichment built for Shopping — decomposed titles, deep categories, clean variants — is exactly the data an AI agent needs to read. Nothing here is throwaway; it all compounds toward the AI-readiness work in chapter ten.

The heavy structural work — 112 category-mapping passes, Range Completion, Hero Sizes, and the CL0–CL4 label architecture — is banked. That's the base every future test and every AI-readiness gain is built on; it doesn't have to be redone.

The invested-hours story

The non-billable share held steady across all three calendar years — 35% in 2024, 33% in 2025, 38% in 2026 — this isn't a one-off gesture, it's how the account has always run.

Disapproval firefighting, urgent sale line-lists, Black Friday keyword pushes and feed-break fixes are the bulk of it: the work that protects live revenue but rarely lands on an invoice.

The honest average

Across the 24 keyword tests with a clean read-out, the average uplift was +12.4% impressions and +11.5% clicks, rising to +22.8% / +22.3% across the 18 that won. Three quarters of keyword tests moved the needle up.

Keyword optimisation is the single most-tested and most-reliable lever on the account — and the one with the clearest, repeatable return.

⚑ Why the average isn't a bigger number

Four seasonal launches (Robes, Jackets Sale, Summer Sale, Football tees) show readings of +165% to +14,000% because they activated near-dormant SKUs from an almost-zero baseline. Those are real activations, but averaging them in would be dishonest — so they're shown separately, not folded into the +12% headline.

The brand-in-title finding — it depends on the category

Two controlled A/B tests ran the same question — does dropping "Superdry" from the title help? The answer split by category:

T-shirts +27.8% clicks — brand-free title won
Dresses −8.0% — keeping the brand won

A genuinely useful, non-obvious result: freeing title space for descriptive keywords helps commodity lines (tees), but the Superdry name still pulls its weight on fashion-led lines (dresses). Title strategy should be set per category, not globally.

Chapter eleven

Case studies & comparable wins

The same pattern, proven across FeedSpark's other programmes.

Proof

Fuller, more decomposable data wins — everywhere we've tested it

Superdry's own keyword results (+12% average, +40–55% on the best batches) sit on a much longer line. Across FeedSpark's client base, the same enrichment thesis returns the same direction — often at far larger magnitudes.

Spelling it out

+126.6%

"Multivitamins" beat "Multivits" on CTR in a controlled title test. Writing the full word out — not the abbreviation — is the single largest title win FeedSpark has recorded.

YuMOVE · Google Shopping

Naming the benefit

+24.7%

Putting the product benefit into the title — not just the product name — lifted CTR. The direct analogue for Superdry: fit, material and use-case in the title, not just "Superdry Jacket".

YuMOVE · benefit copy test

More specific matching

+30%

Adding multiple qualifying terms (multiple conditions) to titles lifted CTR — the more specific the match signal, the better it performs. Superdry's category-keyword work is the same lever.

YuMOVE · keyword specificity

Why these transfer to Superdry

These are different categories — supplements, not apparel — but the mechanism is identical: every attribute you add gives the ranking (and now the AI) engine more to match against. Superdry's own tests already show the same direction; the cross-client results say how much headroom is left when the enrichment goes deeper.

Superdry's own results, in context

+12% avg keyword uplift (24 tests)
✓ Brand-at-end title win
✓ Dynamic sale tags shipped
98% Hero-size coverage

The wins are real and directional; the cross-client magnitudes show what a deeper enrichment programme — descriptions, conversational attributes, MASK titles — could unlock next.
